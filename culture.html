Emirati Culture

Emirati culture is deeply rooted in Islamic values, family bonds, and hospitality. Respect for elders, generosity to guests, and strong community ties are central to daily life.

Traditional Clothing

Kandura and Abaya

The kandura is a traditional men’s garment worn across the Arabian Gulf. Typically white, it reflects heat and offers comfort in warm climates. Known for its simple, flowing design, the kandura represents elegance, modesty, and cultural pride, often worn for both daily life and special occasions.

The abaya is a graceful outer garment worn by women, usually black, symbolizing modesty and sophistication. Designed to flow loosely, it combines tradition with modern style and is often paired with a hijab. The abaya reflects cultural heritage while allowing personal expression through subtle details and tailoring.

Hospitality

Emirati Coffee

Hospitality lies at the heart of Emirati culture. Guests are traditionally welcomed with Arabic coffee (gahwa) and dates as a symbol of respect, generosity, and warmth. The gentle pouring of coffee and the offering of dates reflect centuries-old traditions, where making a guest feel honored and at home is considered a cherished value.

Diversity

Home to over 200+ nationalities

Over 200 nationalities live in the UAE, creating a rich and dynamic multicultural society. People from all over the world come together, bringing with them diverse cultures, languages, and traditions that coexist in harmony. This diversity is reflected in everyday life—from cuisine and celebrations to workplaces and communities—making the UAE a unique place where different backgrounds are respected and celebrated while united by shared values of tolerance and mutual understanding.
